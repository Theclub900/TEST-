About Second Chance Meals

Second Chance Meals is a registered 501(c)(3) non-profit organization dedicated to reducing food waste while feeding our community's most vulnerable members.

Every day, grocery stores, restaurants, and food suppliers throw away perfectly good food simply because it's nearing its sell-by date or has minor cosmetic imperfections. We rescue this food and give it a second chance.

We partner with local businesses to collect food that would otherwise end up in landfills, and transform it into nutritious meals served through our soup kitchens and food drives. Our mission is simple: no good food should go to waste when people in our community are hungry.

Since our founding, we've rescued over 100,000 pounds of food and served thousands of meals to families in need.

How We Rescue Food

1. Food Recovery

We partner with grocery stores, restaurants, and food suppliers to collect surplus food that would be discarded.

2. Safe Preparation

Our volunteers sort, prepare, and cook rescued food following strict food safety guidelines.

3. Community Distribution

We serve meals through soup kitchens and food drives, ensuring everyone has access to nutritious food.

Become a Volunteer

Whether you have a few hours or can commit to regular volunteering, we need your help to rescue food and serve our community.

Volunteer opportunities include:

  • Food Recovery - Pick up donated food from local stores and restaurants
  • Kitchen Prep - Sort, wash, and prepare rescued food
  • Cooking - Help prepare meals in our soup kitchen
  • Serving - Distribute meals and assist guests at food drives
  • Food Safety - Ensure all rescued food meets safety standards
  • Outreach - Connect with businesses willing to donate surplus food
